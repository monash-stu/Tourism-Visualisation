Number of Visitors per State in 2024

Prior to the virus outbreak, the number of visitors was increasing steadily throughout the years. The number of visitors plummeted in 2020-2021 due to COVID-19, and while the recovery was significant, it had yet to reach pre-pandemic highs. Now, in 2024, tourism is more concentrated in the more populous and coastal states, suggesting stronger infrastructure, urban attractions, and natural resources in these areas compared to remote regions. While major states such as New South Wales dominate in sheer numbers, smaller states have seen percentage growth, reflecting broader interest in niche or emerging tourist destinations.

Total expenditure amongst states in 2024

In 2019, visitor numbers peaked across major states like New South Wales (NSW), Victoria (VIC), and Queensland (QLD). However, by 2024, most states see a decline. This drop is especially visible in states like VIC and QLD, which further emphasizes that tourism has yet to be fully back to pre-pandemic highs. Following the trend in visitors, those same states with higher number of visitors are also leading in expenditures, with NSW having the highest expenditure at $11,809. Amongst these visitors, the vast majority of arrivals in 2024 are short-term visitors.

Why do people visit Australia in 2024?

As observed, most visitors arrive at Australia for the purpose of Holiday or Visiting friends/family. It can be concluded that the main drive for Australia's short term visits is Holiday. However, it is also important to note that education and employment opportunities likely contribute to the smaller, but important, long-term arrival category.
